Joseph Pulitzer

1847-1911

Hungarian-American politician and newspaper publisher

A leading national figure in the Democratic Party, who was elected congressman from New York. He crusaded against big business and corruption. He was an establisher of the annual prizes for literature, drama, music and journalism.

The most well-known photo of J.Pulitzer

The following list is a time of Pulizer's life

  • 1847 - was born as Pulitzer József (name order by Hungarian custom) in Makó, about 200 km south-east of Budapest.
  • 1864 - was recruited in Hamburg, Germany, to fight for the Union in the American Civil War.
  • 1868 - Pulitzer was admitted to the bar and got a job of reporter in "Westliche Post"
  • 1870 - voted in favor of the adoption of the Fifteenth Amendment and led a crusade to reform the corrupt St. Louis County Court
  • 1878 - married with Kate Davis at the Episcopal Church of the Epiphany in Washington. The Pulitzers had seven children together: Ralph, Lucille, Katherine, Joseph Jr., Edith, Constance, and Herbert.
  • 1883 - he bought the newspaper "New York World" from Jay Gould.
  • 1885 - Pulitzer was elected as a Democrat to the forty-ninth Congress and served until 1886.
  • 1887 - Pulitzer recruited Nellie Bly, a journalist, who pioneered the idea of investigative reporting by writing articles about poverty, housing and labour conditions in New York.
  • 1896 - the New York World began producing a colour supplement, Richard F. Outcault created a new young character that wore a yellow nightshirt.
  • 1911 - died after eyesight disease aboard his yacht in the harbor of Charleston, S.C..
